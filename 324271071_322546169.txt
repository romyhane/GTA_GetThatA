Project Description:
--------------------
"Get That A" is a collaborative console-based text adventure game where two players, 
Romy (R) and Tomer (T), must work together to solve riddles, avoid bombs, 
open doors, push obstacles, and navigate through levels to defeat the semester.

Changes to Suggested Characters:
--------------------------------
Players: Instead of using '$' and '&', we used 'R' and 'T' (our initials) to make 
the game feel more personal. 

Walls: Instead of using 'W', we designed the walls using '+' for corners, 
'-' for the floor and ceiling, and '|' for vertical walls, creating a cleaner and 
more organized aesthetic.

Monsters: We added a FAIL Monster represented by the character 'F' (see the BONUS 
file for more details).

Darkness: We used the '~' character to represent the Dark Area (Fog) in Level 3.

Map Format & File Validation Logic:
-----------------------------------
To ensure game stability and prevent crashes due to corrupted files, we implemented 
a robust file loading mechanism in the 'initLevel' and 'loadLevelFromFile' functions:

1. Mandatory Legend Anchor ('L'): 
   The game strictly validates that every map file contains the 'L' character. 
   This anchor is critical for aligning the Status Bar (Legend) relative to the board. 
   If 'L' is missing, the system identifies the file as corrupt and refuses to load it.

2. Integrity Check (Empty/Partial Files): 
   The loader counts the actual number of processed lines. If a file exists but 
   is empty or contains insufficient data (e.g., a "cut-off" map), the game detects 
   the anomaly and aborts the launch with a specific error message, instead of 
   loading a broken state.

Key Map Symbols:
----------------
 'R', 'T'       : Player Spawn Points.
 'L'            : Legend/UI Anchor (Must exist!).
 '+', '-', '|'  : Walls (Custom design for corners and sides).
 'F'            : Fail Monster.
 '~'            : Dark Floor Area.
 '\' , '/'		: Regular Switch (as required).
 '^'			: Smart Switch (our addition).
 [Standard game symbols apply for Keys, Doors, Bombs, etc.]

Level 1 Walkthrough:
--------------------
Tomer collects the bomb and detonates it against the wall to reach the Smart Switch. 
He then stands on the switch to open Door #1, allowing Romy to pass through. 
Once Tomer steps off the switch, the door closes. Next, Romy stands on the Smart Switch 
(from the other side) to reopen Door #1, allowing Tomer to join her. 
One player solves the riddle to clear the path, followed by the other. 
Finally, one player collects the key and opens Door #2. 
Both players exit through the door and proceed to Level 2.


Level 2 Walkthrough:
--------------------
Romy and Tomer start near the entrance from the previous level. One player collects 
the key and opens Door #3. Next, one player stands on the Smart Switch to open Door #4, 
allowing the other player to pass. The first player steps off (closing the door), 
and the player who passed stands on the corresponding Smart Switch on the other side 
to reopen Door #4 for the first player. Then, they proceed to solve the riddle. 
After solving the riddle, one player collects a bomb and uses it to destroy the wall 
separating them from the Torch. Next, they collect the Torch, enter the Dark Room, 
and activate the Regular Switch (Latching). This permanently opens Door #5, allowing 
both players to exit to the next level.


Level 3 Walkthrough:
--------------------
Romy and Tomer begin in a closed area blocked by walls and obstacles. To exit, they 
must work together to push a 2-unit Obstacle (**), as their individual default strength 
is 1. Next, they face a heavier 5-unit Obstacle (*****). One player pushes normally 
while the other uses the Spring (####) opposite the obstacle, combining their force (1 + 4) 
to move the block. They then use co-op mechanics on a Smart Switch to open Door #6. 
Upon reaching the Dark Area (marked by '~'), they activate a Regular Switch to open 
Door #7 and collect the Torch (!). To retrieve the Key, one player enters slightly 
into the dark area with the Torch to illuminate the path and reveal the patrolling 
'FAIL Monster' ('F'). Avoiding the monster is critical, as any contact results in an 
immediate GAME OVER and sends you back to the Main Menu! While avoiding it, the 
second player navigates the lit path to the top-left corner to retrieve the Key. 
Finally, they use the key to open Door #8, escaping the level to the end room and 
the Victory Screen.
